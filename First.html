7 Cs of Communication

What are the 7 Cs of communication?

A checklist that helps to improve professional communication skills and increases the
chance that the message will be understood in exactly the same way as it was intended.

This checklist applies to both written and oral communication.

The 7 Cs of communication represent a set of principles that can be applied to improve
communication skills in any setting.

The 7 Cs of communication refer to a model developed by Robert B. Cialdini in his book
"Influence:The Psychology of Persuasion.

Key of the 7 Cs of Communication.

1.Clear ( साफ़ ).

The message should be clear and easily understandable.

It's important to be clear about the purpose of the message you're delivering.

It's essential that the content of the communication be clear. You should avoid jargon,use simple
language, and focus on the points of your Message.

2.Correct ( सही ).

There are no grammatical errors in communication.

Use the most suitable language for your specific message.

If your audience spots errors,they will be distracted, and this will reduce the effectiveness of your communication.

3.Complete ( पूरा ).

The message must include all the relevant information as required by the intended audience.

A complete communication always gives additional information wherever required.
It leaves no questions in the mind of the receiver.

4.Concise ( कम शब्द में ).

It's important to stick to the point and keep your message short and simple.

The sender should avoid lengthy sentences and try to convey the subject matter in the shortest possible words.

Short sentences are likely to keep the attention of the listener.

5.Concrete ( ठोस ).

There is no room for misinterpretation.

Message should be supported with specific facts and figures.

6.Courteous ( विनम्र ).

You can increase the effectiveness of your communication by being polite and showing your audience that
you respect them.

Courtesy implies taking into consideration both viewpoints as well as the feelings of the receiver.

7.Consider(विचार करना).

The sender must take into consideration the receiver's opinions, knowledge, mindset, background, etc. in
order to have effective communication.

The sender must relate to the target recipient and be involved.

Conclusion.

Communication has become a critical skill. The 7 Cs of communication are essential for
career growth and day-to-day life as they enhance one's ability to convey ideas effectively,
build positive relationships, avoid misunderstandings, and present oneself professionally,
contributing to successful interactions and advancement in both personal and professional spheres.
When we communicate well, we become more efficient. tend to command respect among our peers, and we
maintain a healthy relationship with our colleagues.
